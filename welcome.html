1 Preservation of numerical data and OAIS - Open archival Information System

The preservation of digital data is a very important issue for the implementation of the GeOrchestra platform and has to be discussed and thought during its establishment. A standard called OAIS (Open Archival Information System) has been developed for long term preservation.

1.1 OAIS

The Open Archival Information system is a conceptual model for management, archive and preservation of numerical data. Its goal is to preserve information and make it available to a designated community. The information being maintained has been deemed to need long term preservation where long term is long enough to be concerned with the impacts of changing technologies, including support for new media and data formats, or with a changing user community. The OAIS proposes a standardized system that describes the archival system structure of the data.

1.2 Certification and guidelines

Several organisms give certification among which the data seal of approval. This organism gives some guidelines that are very helpful for the implementation and development of the tools needed to be certified. The guidelines are the following:

1. The data producer deposits the research data in a data repository with sufficient information for others to assess the scientific and scholarly quality of the research data and compliance with disciplinary and ethical norms.

2. The data producer provides the research data in formats recommended by the data repository.

3. The data producer provides the research data together with the metadata requested by the data repository.

4. The data repository has an explicit mission in the area of digital archival and promulgates it.

5. The data repository uses due diligence to ensure compliance with legal regulations and contracts.

6. The data repository applies documented processed and procedures for managing data storage.

7. The data repository has a plan for long-term preservation of its digital assets.

8. Archiving takes place according to explicit workflows across the data life cycle.

9. The data repository assumes responsibility from the data producers for access to and availability of the digital objects.

10. The data repository enables the users to utilize the research data and refer to them.

11. The data repository ensures the integrity of the digital objects and the metadata.

12. The data and repository ensures the authenticity of the digital objects and the metadata.

13. The technical infrastructure explicitly supports the task and functions described in internationally accepted archival standards like OAIS.

14. The data consumer must comply with access regulations set by the data repository.

15. The data consumer conforms to and agrees with any codes of conduct that are generally accepted in higher education and research for the exchange and proper use of knowledge and information.

16. The data consumer respects the applicable licenses of the data repository regarding the use of the research data.

The present document describes the different tools/workflow that should be implemented to guarantee the certification OAIS/ data Seal of Approval.

2 Objectives

2.1 Scientific purpose

This project is funded by the labex G-EAU-THERMIE PROFONDE. Its goal is to develop the knowledge in the deep geothermal field by mixing academic and industrial competences and to study the structure and the function of the deep geothermal reservoir of the Upper Rhine Graben area. With the EGS pilot plant of Soultz-sous-Forets, more than 20 years of knowledge have been collected. The "Centre de données de géothermie profonde" (the Data center of deep geothermal energy) has been created in order to preserve and promote the data. GeOrchestra is an open source spatial data infrastructure (SDI) that will be used in order to develop the diffusion platform. This tool will be used as a data repository throughout the GeoNetwork software.

2.2 Data

The data implemented in the platform are any data that has been collected in the Upper Rhine Graben area that could give useful information for a geothermal study. That could be hydraulic, seismology, seismicity, geologic, geodesic or geophysics data
